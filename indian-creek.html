Indian Creek Cycle Rentals

A capstone web application for a family bike-rental business — built, tested, and deployed with a development team, then handed a full production life: hosting, domain, and ongoing support.

View live site ↗ View on GitLab ↗

The Problem

A family-run bike rental business needed a real production web application — one that could list an inventory of 30+ bikes, guide customers through 19 trails, take reviews, and hold up under actual customer traffic. As a capstone, the team needed to work like a real dev shop: design, build, test, and ship, then keep it alive.

How I Solved It

  • Worked within a development team on design, testing, deployment, and ongoing support
  • Identified, documented, and verified fixes for application defects
  • Used GitLab for source control across the project
  • Worked directly with SQL databases through development
  • Owned hosting and domain configuration, and tested functionality across pages and devices post-launch

Technologies

  • SQL
  • GitLab
  • HTML/CSS/JS
  • QA Testing
  • Hosting & DNS

What I Learned

How a feature moves from a ticket to something a real customer clicks on — and how much of "quality" happens after launch, in the testing, monitoring, and small fixes that keep a live site trustworthy.
